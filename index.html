Rules of conduct - a rather relative thing. What is considered indecent in one country, completely normal in another, and vice versa. There is a Korean word for "ejol", translated into Russian as "good breeding, politeness, culture." But these words do not fully convey the meaning of this concept. Even Koreans understand “ejol” differently. It is difficult to define it in one word, because it expresses national tradition and customs.

Korean communication


As in any Asian country, Koreans bow very much: when they greet or say goodbye, ask for a favor, thank you for something or express their respect. It is necessary to treat seniors with respect, not to interrupt. Junior always bows first. The depth of the bow is determined by the difference in the status of the interlocutors: the larger it is, the lower the slope of the younger one and vice versa. If the social status of the interlocutor is high, special respect is expressed by a bow to the floor. Also in Korea, handshakes are widespread. The first is the one who surpasses social status. The one who is lower in status must shake hands with the other, preferably with his two and bow. In Korea, it is not at all customary to do something with one hand, only with two. Therefore, earlier in the old days, they claimed that they did not carry weapons with them and did not want to cause harm...

Korean Job


Business etiquette in South Korea instructs local people to negotiate aggressively. Koreans always want to get results, so they will not beat around the bush for a long time, but prefer to immediately begin to discuss the terms of the deal. They form a position clearly, but they will not try to prove to the interlocutor that he is wrong. You should not use phrases such as "we need time to think." It will be perceived as if you are absolutely not satisfied with the proposed conditions. Communication with potential partners should be arranged according to their status. Treat elders with the utmost respect, respect the hierarchy. If you do not show respect, a Korean may consider it your unwillingness to negotiate with him...

Korean feast


In the Republic, they most often eat sitting on the floor on special pillows. For serving dishes, small low tables are used where the dishes are placed. Before the start of the meal, it is customary to serve the guests a hot, wet towel that wipes their hands. Then the owner puts dishes with food, seasonings.
